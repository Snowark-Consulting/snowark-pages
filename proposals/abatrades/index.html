The framework

Constraint, then more, then better, then new

Every business has one binding constraint. The engine does not matter if you can only build one chassis a day. Find it, fix it, and the whole business speeds up.

1. Find the constraint. Paul's is marketing attribution. Twenty-four Google Ads running and nobody can trace a dollar to a job. Until that is fixed, every other decision is a guess.

2. More. Once we can see what is working, we double down. Same channels, more volume, driven by data — not instinct. Before adding anything new, exhaust what is already converting.

3. Better. Improve the effectiveness of those same channels. Quoting automation lifts conversion without a dollar of new ad spend. Better hooks, better follow-up, higher yield per lead.

4. New. Only after more and better are maxed out. In-house content, SEO, founder-led brand. These are Phase 3 plays — not the starting line.

AI accelerates every step. Surface Google Ads insights for more. Automate quoting for better. Generate content for new. But AI follows the strategy — it does not lead it.

At every step: measure. Then solve the constraint and earn a larger one. Attribution fixed? The bottleneck shifts to operations — how fast can a quote become a completed job? The cycle never ends.

How we would tackle it

Three phases, stage-gated

Phase 1

Find the fastest money · Weeks 1 to 4

On site from day one. Goal: identify at least two ROI-positive bolt-ons.

  • Unify marketing attribution: consolidate GA4 across domains, stand up a single dashboard with cost-per-acquired-job by channel
  • Mine phone call data: customer insights engine live within the month
  • Surface Google Ads intelligence: anomaly detection and performance briefs before every agency meeting
  • Map workflows across all four service lines: where does paper stop? What gets re-entered?

Gate: you sign off on the Phase 2 roadmap before any build starts.

Phase 2

First wins · Weeks 5 to 12

Shift into builder mode. On-site tapers as automations go live.

  • Deploy the highest-ROI bolt-on from Phase 1, likely quoting automation across all four service lines
  • At least one workflow running autonomously end to end
  • Ship a revenue-positive A/B test against a manual baseline
Phase 3

Scale · Months 3 to 6

  • Deploy across all four service lines
  • Extend AI capability where you decide it adds leverage
  • Knowledge transfer: your team operates and maintains
  • Living agent registry, quarterly reviews
