The framework

Constraint, then more, then better, then new

Every business has one binding constraint. Twenty-nine vans on the road, but if you can only quote so many jobs a day, the rest are parked. Find it, fix it, and the whole business speeds up.

1. Find the constraint. Paul has two. Marketing attribution is the obvious one: nobody can trace spend to a job. The shadow constraint is operations: ServiceTitan holds the data but presents it poorly. GP per job, technician efficiency, timesheet accuracy. Paul can see the problems on the ground, but the numbers do not tell the same story. Until both constraints are visible, both are guesses.

2. More. Once we can see what is working, we double down. Same channels, more volume, driven by data, not instinct. Before adding anything new, exhaust what is already converting.

3. Better. Improve the effectiveness of those same channels. Quoting automation lifts conversion without a dollar of new ad spend. Better hooks, better follow-up, higher yield per lead.

4. New. Only after more and better are maxed out. In-house content, SEO, founder-led brand. These are Phase 3 plays.

AI accelerates every step. Surface Google Ads insights for more. Automate quoting for better. Generate content for new. But AI follows the strategy, it does not lead it.

At every step: measure. Then solve the constraint and earn a larger one. Attribution and operations visibility solved? The bottleneck shifts: how fast can a quote become a completed job? That is the next loop. The cycle never ends.

How we would tackle it

Three phases, stage-gated

Phase 1

Find the constraint, then more · Weeks 1 to 4

On site from day one. Goal: identify the binding constraint and do more of what is already working, with data.

  • Find the constraint. Unify marketing attribution: stand up a single dashboard with cost-per-acquired-job by channel. The constraint is almost certainly here. We prove it with data.
  • More. Surface Google Ads intelligence: anomaly detection and performance briefs before every agency meeting. Double down on what is already converting once we can see it.
  • Mine two years of C3X phone call recordings: customer insights engine live within the month. Understand what buyers actually say before they book, and where leads are lost.
  • Audit operations: extract ServiceTitan data, map GP tracking per job and per technician, identify where reporting breaks down between the field and the P&L.
  • Map workflows across all four service lines: where does paper stop? What gets re-entered?

Gate: you sign off on the Phase 2 roadmap before any build starts.

Phase 2

Better · Weeks 5 to 12

The Phase 1 audit tells us exactly what to build. Shift into builder mode. On-site tapers as automations go live. Same channels, higher yield.

Examples of what typically emerges from the audit:

  • Better conversion. If the constraint is quoting, deploy automation across all four service lines. Same leads, higher close rate. A two to three percent lift is conservative and covers the engagement three to five times over.
  • Better visibility. If the constraint is operations, stand up automated GP reporting, GPS timesheet integration, a technician leaderboard. Surface what the field already knows in a format the leadership team can run with.
  • At least one workflow running autonomously end to end, with AI accelerating every step.
  • Ship a revenue-positive A/B test against a manual baseline. Measure everything.
