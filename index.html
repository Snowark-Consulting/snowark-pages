The problem

Four ways it quietly compounds

Fragmented data

Your CRM, project tools, spreadsheets, and inbox don’t talk to each other. Every decision rests on a hunch rather than a single source of truth.

02

Email follow-ups, data entry, quoting, reporting. The work that keeps the business running but keeps you from building it.

03

Things falling through cracks

Leads go cold. Quotes sit unapproved. Clients wait for updates. No one has a complete view of what needs attention right now.

Competitors pulling ahead

While you’re working in the business, others are adopting AI to reduce costs, close more deals, and move faster. The gap is widening. You can feel it.

Fair questions

The things people actually ask.

Is this just ChatGPT with extra steps?

No. A chatbot waits for you to type. Your agent has persistent memory, connects to your actual tools, and works to a schedule whether you’re at your desk or not. The difference shows up at 6am, when the morning report is already written.

Is Snowark really one person?

One operator, plus his agents, and that’s the point. You get the person who built the infrastructure, on every engagement. The heavy lifting runs overnight because my own agents do it. And everything built for you is documented and owned by you, so nothing depends on me being awake.

What happens to my data?

It stays in your systems. The agent connects to your platforms under credentials you control and can revoke at any time. Nothing is pooled, resold, or used to train anything.

What if the agent makes a mistake?

It will, occasionally, which is why nothing external leaves without sign-off. Agents draft, humans approve. Trust is widened gradually, task by task, exactly as you’d do with a new hire.

How long before it’s actually useful?

Deployed in days, useful in the first week, and compounding from there. The agent gets more valuable as its context builds. Month three beats month one comfortably.

What if we stop working together?

You keep everything. Your agent, your infrastructure, your integrations, your documentation. We run it while you need us. You own it forever.

Do I need technical staff?

No. If your team can use the tools they already use, they can work with the agent. We handle the plumbing.

Can I talk to the agent myself?

Yes, and you should. It’s not a black box we operate behind a curtain. It’s a colleague you message, question, and redirect. Most clients are surprised how quickly that becomes normal.
