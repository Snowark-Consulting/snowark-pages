What founders try first

None of it quite works

Before Snowark, most founders try one of these paths. Each solves part of the problem. None solves the whole thing.

An offshore virtual assistant

Still a person, still constrained by time. You trade one set of management overhead for another, and the context never quite accumulates the way it should.

Zapier and point solutions

Powerful, but they demand you understand every workflow up front. They automate what you already know to automate. They do not surface what you are missing.

Doing it yourself with ChatGPT

The learning curve is steep and the output is shallow. A chatbot does not have persistent memory, does not connect to your tools, and does not run while you sleep.

A freelancer from Upwork or Fiverr

Short-term resource, no business context. They build on flimsy hosting and disappear when things break. The IP walks out the door with them.

How we work

Business strategy first, AI second

Most AI vendors start with the tool and look for a problem. We start with the P&L. We find the bottleneck, then point the AI at it. Here is how it works.

  1. 01

    Map your operations

    We learn your workflows, your tools, and where the friction lives. We identify the single biggest constraint on your growth: awareness, conversion, or operations. We research your stack before we even meet.

  2. 02

    Configure your agent

    Using pre-built infrastructure, we deploy an agent that plugs into your existing tools and starts working at the highest-leverage point. Not a generic chatbot. A configured business partner that understands your context and acts on it.

  3. 03

    Run and refine

    We do not hand over and walk away. We operate your agent month to month, improving its capability and pointing it at the next constraint. You focus on your clients. The agent handles the rest.
