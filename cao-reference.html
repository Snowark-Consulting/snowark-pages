2. Pain Points

Operational

  1. Four service lines, one manual operation. Plumbing, electrical, roofing, and solar each carry their own scheduling, quoting, and compliance requirements. Manual coordination across all four creates bottlenecks at every handoff.
  2. Quoting and job management is time-intensive. Quote generation across multiple trade types, job tracking, and compliance documentation are high-volume, rules-based tasks consuming significant bandwidth daily.
  3. Compliance burden. Trade businesses carry heavy compliance obligations. Document processing, licensing tracking, and job record management eat hours of admin per week.

Marketing

  1. Marketing ROI is unclear. Investment across multiple digital channels with no clear attribution from spend to booked jobs and revenue.
  2. Heavy agency dependency. Digital marketing and reporting currently outsourced. Goal is to progressively build in-house capability, reducing cost and dependency.

Systems

  1. No unified layer. ServiceTitan, Xero, Podium, WageLock, Asset Panda all operate in silos. Manual reconciliation between systems.
  2. AI-ready founder, no systems yet. Paul has shown up twice for initial calls despite connection issues, genuine intent. The gap is finding the right operator to design and build the automation layer end-to-end.

3. Growth Constraint Framework

The rule of growth: when lead and growth marketing stall, there are only three possible bottlenecks. Find which one is binding, fix it, then find the next one.

1
Awareness
Are we getting enough leads?
Channel mix, spend levels, reach
2
Conversion
Are we converting the leads we get?
Close rates, quote quality, sales process
3
Operations
How fast are we getting through the back end?
Booking → completion time, system friction

How to diagnose: The first thing is understanding the data. What data are we collecting around lead awareness, conversions, and back-end process? Once we have the data, we determine the baselines and where the bottlenecks are. If we don't have the data, that is where we start: how do we collect it?

The Sequencing Rule

If awareness and conversion are working but growth is still slow, the constraint is in operations: how quickly are we getting from booking through to job completion? Is there time wasted in the systems we are using?

For a multi-trade business, operations constraint is highly likely. Four service lines, manual quoting/scheduling, compliance overhead, the back end is where friction compounds.

Working Backwards from Business Goals

If the goal is X thousand jobs in the next year:

  1. How many leads are needed, given current conversion rate, for that to happen?
  2. Where are those leads coming from currently?
  3. What channels are actually working?
  4. If we don't have tracking in place, that is step one, get baselines to see what's working and what's not.

Core principle: Do not optimise anything in isolation. Understand what is going to move the needle for the business goals. Critical numbers are the measure of success.

Tracking Checklist

  • Google Ads conversion tracking, firing on form submissions and phone calls
  • Meta Pixel, installed, firing, with standard events (Lead, Contact, Schedule)
  • GA4 configured correctly, channel grouping, conversions defined, no gaps
  • Microsoft Clarity installed, session recordings for qualitative insight
  • UTM hygiene, all paid traffic consistently tagged with source/medium/campaign
  • Call tracking, phone calls attributed to the channel that generated them
  • CRM lead source tracking, ServiceTitan recording lead source so jobs can be traced to channel
  • Dashboard, single view: spend → leads → booked jobs → revenue, per channel. Plus customer LTV, retention, and repeat rate.

Likely State of Play

The brief says "marketing ROI is unclear" and "heavy reliance on external agencies." This almost certainly means:

  • Agencies are reporting on their own performance (clicks, impressions, spend) with no connection to actual jobs booked
  • The website likely has incomplete or broken conversion tracking
  • No UTM discipline across campaigns
  • ServiceTitan is probably not being used as a lead source tracker
  • No customer insight work has been done, no one knows who the best customer is
  • Paul is making marketing decisions on incomplete data

The Fix (What Josh Proposes)

  1. Audit the tracking stack, 2–3 days. Map every pixel, tag, and conversion event. Fix what's broken.
  2. Get the baselines, GA4 + Clarity + agent synthesis. Understand what channels are working before changing anything.
  3. Wire ServiceTitan to attribution, every booked job carries a lead source. This is the missing link between marketing spend and revenue.
  4. Run customer insight analysis, best customer, LTV, retention, JTBD. Over the CRM.
  5. Build a single dashboard, one place for spend, leads, jobs, revenue, cost per acquisition, and customer metrics. Updated automatically.
  6. Then, and only then, decide which channels to keep outsourced and which to bring in-house. Data first, decisions second.

8. Interview Questions, Josh's Anchors

Q1: "Walk me through the most complex AI system you've built and deployed in a real business."

Not Forgotten Systems (Atlas). Automated quote follow-up for trades. Quote emails → agent processes → follow-up sequences → owner notified on response. Built end-to-end: knowledge base scraping, agent deployment on orgo.ai, Telegram + email channels, dashboard generation, AI avatar video content via HeyGen. Recovered $150k+ in attributed revenue. The Snowark deploy kit was designed to handle multi-service operations with scheduling, quoting, and compliance out of the box, this is not a custom build per client, it's infrastructure.

Q2: "If you were starting here on Monday, what's the first thing you'd want to understand?"

The data. What data are we collecting around lead awareness, conversions, and back-end process? The rule of growth says there are only three possible constraints: awareness, conversion, or operations. I need to see which one is binding. If we don't have the data, that's where I start, how do we collect it, what does the baseline look like? I also need to understand the business strategy: what are the key priorities that move the needle? I don't optimise anything in isolation. Critical numbers are the measure of success.

Then I walk the workflow, how a lead becomes a job and how a job becomes cash, with the person who does it, for each service line. I want to see where the friction lives, where things get re-entered, where the paper stops.

Q3: "How would you approach mapping workflows across four service lines in two weeks?"

One service line per 2–3 days. Shadow the person who does the work. Document the steps. Identify where the same logic repeats across lines, quoting structure, compliance checks, scheduling rules are likely the same pattern with different line items. The goal is not four separate automations; it's one automation layer with four configurations.

Q4: "How would you assess whether to bring marketing in-house vs. keeping it outsourced?"

I have done both and I still use a mix. Media buying, Google Ads in particular, I would probably outsource to an agency. Being an expert day in, day out across multiple brands is an advantage for ad placement, they do not need deep business knowledge to be good at it. Social media is different: it needs to be close to the business, based on core principles, deeply entwined. That should be in-house. Videography, probably part-time in-house.

But all of this depends on the baseline. I need to understand what channels are currently working before I recommend changing anything. If we don't have tracking in place, that's step one. Once we can see cost per acquired job by channel, the build vs. buy decision makes itself.

I wouldn't recommend expensive attribution software like Triple Whale at this stage. GA4 is fine, Microsoft Clarity is good, and getting an agent to synthesise data from those systems is a very effective way to start.

Q5: "What does your first 90 days look like?"

Days 1–14: Understand the data, what's being collected, what's missing, what are the baselines. Map workflows across all four service lines. Run customer insight analysis over the CRM: who is the best customer, what's LTV, what's the retention rate? Understand the business strategy and critical numbers.

Days 15–30: Fix broken tracking. Get GA4 and Clarity properly configured. Deploy agent layer on quoting and scheduling (highest-volume, most rules-based work). Connect to ServiceTitan.

Days 31–60: Build attribution dashboard, spend, leads, jobs, revenue, CPA per channel, plus customer metrics. Connect agent to Xero for invoicing. Begin compliance automation.

Days 61–90: Extend agent to customer comms (Podium). Assess agency performance with actual data. Make informed build vs. buy decisions on marketing channels. Train team on systems. Hand off operational ownership.

Decision rule: Prioritise by what's constraining throughput. If the back end is the bottleneck, fixing quoting and scheduling unlocks more value than a perfect attribution dashboard on a trickle. If awareness is the bottleneck, tracking and channel optimisation comes first. The data tells you which.
